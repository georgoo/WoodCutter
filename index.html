To those~ who dream of a fulfilled life .

It recently occurred to me...

Everyone is familiar with the story of the woodcutter whose axe fell into the river. A goddess appeared with three axes—a golden axe, a silver axe, and an iron axe—and gave him all three as a reward for choosing the right one.

When asked about the moral of this story, most people say it’s about honesty. But there’s more to it. It’s also about the axes—the choices we make.

As children, we naturally gravitate towards certain activities, things we love to do simply because they make us happy. We follow our instincts and pursue these passions without hesitation.

But as we grow older, things change. We try to fit in, bowing to peer pressure and societal expectations. We chase wealth, recognition, and success. In this pursuit, we either lose sight of our passion or ignore it altogether for so many reasons—fear, doubt, or practicality.

Yet, like the axe that sank to the bottom of the river, our passion remains within us, hidden in the depths of our being.

When the goddess presented the woodcutter with the three axes, he chose the right one because he remembered his own axe. He had not forgotten what it looked like because it was etched in his memory.

Similarly, life offers us choices—different "axes." The iron axe represents our passion, the silver axe represents fame, and the golden axe represents wealth. If we choose the right one—our passion—we often gain the others as well, just as the goddess gifted all three axes to the woodcutter.

But for this to happen, we need a memory as clear as the woodcutter’s to recognize our true axe from our past, the one we’ve always had deep inside. And most importantly, we must be honest with ourselves when it comes to choosing.

If you feel lost or unsure of your passion, pause and look back at your life. What were the things you loved? What are the quiet joys that have followed you over the years?
